7 RESIN PLASTIC CODES

The seven resin codes are symbols found on plastic products that indicate the type of plastic material used and its recyclability. Each code, represented by a number inside a triangle of arrows, helps consumers and recyclers identify how to handle the plastic product after use.

1. PET (Polyethylene Terephthalate) Common Uses: Water bottles, soft drink bottles, food jars. Properties: Lightweight, clear, strong, and resistant to moisture. Recyclability: Widely recyclable and often recycled into new bottles or fibers for clothing.

2. HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene) Common Uses: Milk jugs, detergent bottles, juice bottles, grocery bags. Properties: Sturdy, resistant to impact, and moisture. Recyclability: Easily recyclable into new bottles, pipes, or plastic lumber

3. PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride) Common Uses: Plumbing pipes, medical equipment, shrink wrap. Properties: Tough, can be rigid or flexible, and resistant to chemicals. Recyclability: Difficult to recycle due to the release of harmful chemicals when processed. It is generally downcycled into products like flooring.

4. LDPE (Low-Density Polyethylene) Common Uses: Squeezable bottles, bread bags, frozen food packaging. Properties: Flexible, transparent, and resistant to moisture. Recyclability: Less commonly recycled but can be used to create plastic bags, garbage can liners, and floor tiles.

5.PP (Polypropylene) Common Uses: Yogurt containers, bottle caps, straws, automotive parts. Properties: Tough, resistant to heat, and acts as a barrier to moisture. Recyclability: Recyclable into signal lights, battery cables, and trays.

6. PS (Polystyrene) Common Uses: Disposable coffee cups, plastic food boxes, plastic cutlery, packing peanuts. Properties: Lightweight, can be rigid or foam, insulating. Recyclability: Difficult to recycle, often ends up in landfills or is incinerated.
